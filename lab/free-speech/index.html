Free Speech

This is another experimental post. Arguably more ‘experimental’ than the previous ones.

It is simply a visualisation of the most common words submitted here. Everything gets added and nothing is censored. This project did not feel like a novel idea, but surprisingly I could not find anything similar.

Anyway, you can add to the statistics too.

The data includes submissions from anyone who happened to visit this page and add to the count — whether legitimately, or by DoSing the API. Since the database needs to scale to arbitrarily large sizes, it uses some nifty tricks to store the data. This means the data is ‘only’ 99% accurate however.

Accuracy of the data

The database does not store any individual word. Instead, it uses HyperLogLog for cardinality estimation, t-digest to estimate ranks, and a count-min sketch and cuckoo heavy keeper to calculate frequency estimates. The cardinality and rank estimate is 99% accurate. The frequency estimate for the top 10,000 elements is also 99% accurate. However, the frequency estimate for the other words is less than 99%, and may be higher than the true frequency.
